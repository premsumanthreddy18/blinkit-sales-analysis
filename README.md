 Blinkit Grocery Sales Analysis Dashboard | Power BI

Business Problem
Blinkit, India's last-minute grocery delivery app, required visibility 
into sales performance across outlet types, city tiers, and product 
categories to identify revenue drivers and underperforming segments 
and make data-backed expansion and stocking decisions.

 Tools Used
Power BI, DAX, Data Modelling, Power Query

Dataset
Retail grocery sales dataset containing 8,500+ items across multiple 
outlet types, establishment years, and city tiers.

 Key KPIs Tracked
- Total Sales: ₹1.20M
- Average Sales per Item: ₹141
- Number of Items: 8,523
- Average Customer Rating: 3.9
- Sales by Fat Content (Low Fat vs Regular)
- Sales by Item Type (Fruits, Snacks, Household, Dairy etc.)
- Sales by Outlet Size (Small, Medium, High)
- Sales by City Tier (Tier 1, Tier 2, Tier 3)
- Sales by Outlet Type (Supermarket Type 1/2/3, Grocery Store)
- Item Visibility Score by Outlet

 Key Insights
- Tier 3 outlets generated the highest revenue at ₹4.72L despite 
  smaller store count — indicating strong untapped demand in 
  smaller cities
- Supermarket Type 1 dominates total sales at ₹7.87L with 5,577 
  items, significantly ahead of all other outlet types
- Low Fat products outsell Regular Fat products 65% vs 35% — 
  suggesting a health-conscious customer base
- Outlets established in 2018 recorded the highest annual sales 
  at ₹2.05L, showing strong growth trajectory in newer locations
- Average customer rating remains consistent at 3.9 across all 
  outlet types — indicating uniform service quality

Dashboard Features
- Dynamic slicers for Outlet Location Type, Outlet Size, and 
  Item Type enabling multi-dimensional filtering
- Toggle buttons to switch KPI view between Total Sales, 
  Average Sales, Number of Items, and Average Rating
- Matrix table with conditional formatting for outlet-level 
  drill-down across all KPIs
- Line chart showing sales trend by outlet establishment year
